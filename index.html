Four Treasures of the Study

Brush, Ink, Paper and Inkstone, the core tools of traditional Chinese writing and painting, carrying thousands of years of cultural essence

Writing Brush

The writing brush is the first of the Four Treasures of the Study, made of animal hair and bamboo/wood, writing elegant and vivid Chinese characters. Its history can be traced back to the Warring States Period.

A high-quality brush must have the four virtues of "sharpness, neatness, roundness and elasticity".

View Details

Hui Ink Stick

With varying shades of black, ink sticks are made from pine soot or lampblack. Grinding ink and wielding the brush reveals oriental charm. Hui ink from Anhui is world-famous.

High-quality ink sticks have a fine texture, bright black color, and write smoothly without clogging the brush.

View Details

Xuan Paper

Xuan paper has a dense texture and excellent ink absorption, remaining intact for thousands of years, making it ideal for calligraphy and painting. It is known as the "King of Papers".

Made from wingnut bark and sand field rice straw through 18 major processes and hundreds of operations.

View Details

Inkstone

Inkstones are crafted according to the material, with a warm texture and fine ink grinding. They are both practical tools and artistic treasures. The Four Famous Inkstones are world-renowned.

A good inkstone must have fine texture, easy ink production, and no damage to brush hair.

View Details

Four Arts of the Scholar

Qin, Qi, Shu and Hua, the four essential arts for ancient Chinese scholars, reflecting the spiritual pursuit of the Chinese people

Guqin

The guqin has a wide range, deep tone, and lingering sound, being one of the oldest plucked instruments in China, with a history of over 3,000 years.

The sound of guqin is clear, subtle, tranquil and remote, a medium for scholars to cultivate their morality.

View Details

Go (Weiqi)

Go is extensive and profound, containing oriental philosophical wisdom, and is a perfect combination of strategy and thinking. It originated in China over 4,000 years ago.

Go games are not only a contest of skills but also a competition of mentality.

View Details

Calligraphy

Calligraphy is the art of lines, showing the writer's mood and accomplishment through the use of brush and ink. It has gone through many evolutionary stages.

Wang Xizhi's "Lanting Xu" is known as the "best running script under heaven".

View Details

Chinese Painting

Chinese painting emphasizes artistic conception, uses ink wash as the main expression form, and pursues the artistic state of combining spirit and form.

The integration of poetry, calligraphy, painting and seal is the core feature of Chinese painting.

Cultural Essence

The Four Treasures of the Study and the Four Arts are important carriers of traditional Chinese culture, carrying the Chinese people's aesthetic taste, philosophical thoughts and humanistic spirit.

The Four Treasures are the material foundation of cultural creation, and the Four Arts are the artistic forms of spiritual expression. They complement each other and jointly construct the spiritual world of Chinese literati.

Harmony Between Man and Nature

The core thought of traditional Chinese culture, pursuing harmony between man and nature

Self-cultivation

Improving personal moral cultivation and spiritual realm through the practice of skills

Artistic Conception

Pursuing implicit and profound artistic conception, emphasizing "intention before brush"
